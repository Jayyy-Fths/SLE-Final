Frequently Asked Questions

What are the minimum enlistment requirements?

Be 17+ with parental consent (or 18+), pass ASVAB, pass MEPS medical, and have a high school diploma or GED.

How does the split option affect college?

It lets students attend summer basic training after junior year and then complete senior year while drilling monthly.

Can I choose my MOS?

Your preferred MOS depends on ASVAB, physical fitness, medical, and unit needs. We help match you to the best fit.

What education benefits are included?

NJ SMART Tuition Waiver, GI Bill, TA for civilian school, and career training certifications are included in many enlistment plans.

ASVAB & MOS Qualification Guide

General ASVAB Score

The minimum AFQT for NJ Army Guard is generally 31. Some advanced MOS require 60+.

  • 31-49: Combat & Logistics careers
  • 50-69: Engineer and Aviation entry points
  • 70+: Cyber, Intelligence, and Special Operations paths

Key Subscores

Major MOS categories depend on these subtests.

  • GT: General Technical (Intelligence, Cyber)
  • CO: Combat Ops (Infantry, Armor)
  • ST: Skilled Technical (Medical, Logistical)
  • MM: Mechanical Maintenance
  • EL: Electronics

How to improve grades

Show this to your recruiter for MOS counseling.

  • Practice the ASVAB practice tests weekly
  • Use military study guides for targeted subscores
  • Retest after 30 days with practice-based prep

To qualify for a specific MOS, check the entry lines in each card and ask your recruiter to verify your current ASVAB subscore match. If you need an MOS with a unique lower score profile, prioritize options in combat/logistics, then work toward technical and intelligence scores.

ASVAB waiver: Some MOS have waiver paths for limited ASVAB shortfalls; ask your recruiter about waiver eligibility and local unit needs.
