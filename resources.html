Frequently Asked Questions

Can't find your answer? Contact a recruiter.

Can I join with tattoos?

Yes. Tattoos are allowed with restrictions — no tattoos on the face, neck, or hands (ring finger tattoos are allowed). Extremist, indecent, or gang-related tattoos disqualify. A recruiter will review during the screening process.

What is the minimum ASVAB score?

The minimum AFQT (Armed Forces Qualification Test) score for the Army National Guard is 31. However, most technical MOS require composite scores of 85–110. Higher scores unlock more career options. Practice tests are available free at asvabprogram.com.

What is the Split Training Option?

The Split Training Option (STO) lets you complete Basic Combat Training one summer and Advanced Individual Training (AIT) the following summer — so you never miss more than one school year. Perfect for high school seniors and college students.

Will I be deployed overseas?

Possibly. NJ Guard units can be federally mobilized for overseas deployments, but most members serve domestically — responding to natural disasters, state emergencies, and homeland security missions. Deployment frequency varies by unit.

Does the NJ Guard cover college tuition?

Yes — the NJ National Guard Tuition Waiver covers 100% of tuition at any New Jersey public college or university (Rutgers, NJIT, Rowan, community colleges, etc.). This is in addition to federal education benefits like the GI Bill and federal tuition assistance.

Can I keep my current job?

Absolutely. The Guard is designed for part-time service. Your civilian employer cannot legally fire you for Guard duties — you're protected by USERRA (Uniformed Services Employment and Re-employment Rights Act). Most employers are Guard-friendly.

What are the age requirements?

You must be between 17 and 35 years old to enlist. Prior-service members may be able to join up to age 59. Age waivers are possible — speak with a recruiter to explore your options.

What health benefits are included?

Guard members have access to TRICARE Reserve Select — affordable health and dental coverage for yourself and your family. Full TRICARE coverage activates automatically when deployed or on active orders.

How long is the commitment?

The standard enlistment is 8 years total — typically 3–6 years of active Guard service, with remaining time in the Individual Ready Reserve (IRR). Many members re-enlist. Your specific contract depends on your MOS and any bonus agreement.

Can non-citizens join?

Permanent Residents (Green Card holders) can enlist in most MOS. U.S. Citizenship is required for some intelligence and security clearance roles. You must have lived in the U.S. for at least 2 years and speak proficient English.
